US to Supply Depleted Uranium Tank Shells to Ukraine: What You Need to Know

Introduction:
In a recent development, the United States has announced its decision to provide military aid to Ukraine, including controversial depleted uranium tank shells, as part of a larger $1 billion military and humanitarian assistance package. This move has drawn strong condemnation from Russia, which views the decision as a provocative act. In this blog post, we will explore the key aspects of this decision, including what depleted uranium shells are, their potential risks, and the implications of this decision on the ongoing conflict in Ukraine.

**What are Depleted Uranium Shells?**
Depleted uranium shells are ammunition designed for tanks and other armored vehicles. These shells are made from depleted uranium, which is a by-product of the uranium enrichment process. Depleted uranium is stripped of most radioactive materials and is considerably less radioactive than natural uranium, according to the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). It should be noted that depleted uranium cannot generate a nuclear reaction.

**Why Depleted Uranium Shells?**
Depleted uranium is valued for its extreme density and ability to pierce conventional tank armor. These shells are highly effective at penetrating enemy armored vehicles, as they sharpen upon impact and ignite after contact. This feature makes them a preferred choice for armor-piercing ammunition.

**Controversy Surrounding Depleted Uranium Shells:**
The use of depleted uranium shells has been controversial due to concerns about their potential health and environmental effects. While the UN Scientific Committee on the Effects of Atomic Radiation has found no significant poisoning caused by exposure to depleted uranium, the IAEA acknowledges that there could be a risk of radiation to individuals who handle fragments of depleted uranium rounds. Russia, in particular, has expressed strong objections when the UK announced a similar decision to supply depleted uranium shells to Ukraine earlier.

**US Reversal on Sending Depleted Uranium Shells:**
The US decision to supply depleted uranium munitions to Ukraine represents a significant reversal from its previous stance. In March, the Pentagon had stated that it would not send such munitions. However, it is now considered the best way to arm the Abrams tanks scheduled for delivery to Ukraine later this year.

**US Assistance Package for Ukraine:**
In addition to depleted uranium shells, the US will provide Ukraine with other military equipment, including anti-armour systems, tactical air navigation systems, and additional ammunition for Himars missiles. This assistance is aimed at supporting Ukraine in its ongoing conflict with Russia and is part of a broader strategy to bolster Ukraine's defense capabilities.

**Russian Reaction and Ongoing Conflict:**
Russia has strongly criticized the US decision, labeling it as "an indicator of inhumanity." Moscow believes that the Ukrainian military's counter-offensive is failing, while Ukrainian generals claim they have breached Russia's formidable defenses in the south. The conflict continues to be a major point of contention in international relations.

**Conclusion:**
The decision by the United States to supply depleted uranium tank shells to Ukraine is a contentious development in the ongoing conflict with Russia. While these shells are prized for their effectiveness in penetrating armor, concerns about their potential risks persist. The move underscores the complex geopolitical dynamics in the region and the ongoing challenges in finding a resolution to the conflict. The international community will closely monitor the situation as it evolves.
