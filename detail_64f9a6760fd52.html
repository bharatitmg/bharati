One Piece Live-Action Series Dominates Netflix's Global English TV Rankings

Netflix made a big splash with the debut of the Hollywood live-action adaptation of Eiichiro Oda's beloved manga, "One Piece." In its very first week on the streaming service from August 28 to September 3, the series claimed the top spot on Netflix's global English-language television rankings, amassing a whopping 18.5 million total views and 140.1 million hours of viewership. The average viewer spent approximately 7 hours and 34 minutes immersed in the world of "One Piece."

This extraordinary achievement wasn't limited to a single region; the series broke into the top 10 in an impressive 96 countries and emerged as the #1 ranked show in 46 of them. While it secured second place in the United States, it faced tough competition from the "Who is Erin Carter?: Limited Series," which ranked at #2 globally with 90.3 million views, 15.8 million hours viewed, and an average runtime of 5 hours and 43 minutes.

The live-action adaptation, which premiered exclusively on Netflix on August 31, featured eight episodes instead of the originally reported ten. To accompany the series, a captivating soundtrack was released, featuring the main theme "WEALTH FAME POWER," composed by the talented Sonya Belousova and Giona Ostinelli, known for their work on "The Witcher."

In a nod to the original Japanese anime, the Japanese voice actors for Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Usopp, and Sanji reprise their roles in the Japanese dub. Additionally, Iñaki Godoy and Taz Skylar lend their voices to Luffy and Sanji in the Spanish dub.

Produced by Tomorrow Studios, a partnership between renowned producer Marty Adelstein (known for "Prison Break" and "Teen Wolf") and ITV Studios, the live-action series was written by Matt Owens, who boasts credits on shows like "Luke Cage" and "Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D." Steven Maeda and Becky Clements served as executive producers.

Original creator Eiichiro Oda, who also took on an executive producer role, had emphasized the importance of waiting until he was "satisfied" with the project before its launch. He expressed confidence that all parties involved were working harmoniously to deliver an authentic "One Piece" experience to fans.

Back in 2017, Marty Adelstein revealed that the live-action adaptation would kick off with the East Blue arc and gradually expand to cover more of the beloved source material, promising to bring to life the essence of "One Piece" in a way that would do justice to Oda's vision.

The success of the live-action "One Piece" series on Netflix marks a significant milestone in the world of manga and anime adaptations, solidifying its place among the platform's top-tier offerings. With its immense global popularity, it seems that the spirit of adventure and camaraderie that defines "One Piece" continues to resonate with audiences across the world.
